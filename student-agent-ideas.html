Agent Ideas for Students

Exploring what we can build now and in the future with our multi-agent workflow system. These agents eliminate meta-work so students can focus on actual learning.

Why Agent Workflows for Students?

Not doing work for students

These agents eliminate the meta-work (organizing, scheduling, formatting, communicating) so students can focus on the actual work (learning, thinking, creating). A GPS doesn't drive the car — it removes the cognitive load of navigation.

Equity at scale

Many organizational skills (email etiquette, study planning, time management) are informally taught in privileged environments. First-generation students, working students, and students with disabilities disproportionately lack this scaffolding. Agents democratize access.

Progressive disclosure

Start with Tier 1 (prototypable now, low risk, high visibility). Use demos to build stakeholder confidence. Use learnings to inform Tier 2 investment. Tier 3 is the vision that justifies the platform.

How It Works

The multi-agent pub/sub system chains specialized agents together. A manager routes requests, agents execute in parallel or sequence, and results aggregate back to the student.

Tier 1: Buildable Now

Zero Backend Changes Required

These use existing Composio tools, Knowledge Base, and pub/sub architecture. Only configuration needed.

01

Course Prep & Organization

Student says "Set me up for CSE 310" — agent scrapes the course page, creates a Google Drive folder structure, generates doc templates for notes, and builds a master schedule in Sheets.

Firecrawl, Google Drive, Google Sheets, Google Docs
Why this matters Eliminates first-week setup chaos. Reduces the "setup tax" that causes students to fall behind before they start.
02

Study Material Generator

Student provides a topic or chapter — agent pulls content from the KB (synced syllabus), creates a structured study guide in Docs, and generates a review sheet in Sheets with key terms and definitions.

Knowledge Base, Google Docs, Google Sheets
Why this matters Scaffolds learning without replacing it. Particularly valuable for first-generation students who may not have been taught study organization techniques.
03

Group Project Coordinator

Student says "Set up our group project for ENG 102 with 4 members" — agent creates a shared Drive folder, project tracker in Sheets, shared meeting notes Doc, and drafts an intro email.

Google Drive, Google Sheets, Google Docs, Gmail
Why this matters Coordination overhead (not intellectual work) is what derails groups. Especially impactful for online/remote students who lack hallway coordination.
04

Presentation Builder

Student provides an outline or essay draft — agent structures it into Google Slides with logical sections, speaker notes, and a summary slide.

Google Slides, Google Docs
Why this matters Reduces "blank slide" paralysis. Provides a structural starting point that students refine — teaches presentation format through example.
05

Weekly Planning & Review

Student says "Plan my week" — agent reads schedule data, cross-references upcoming deadlines from KB syllabus, creates calendar blocks for study time, and emails a weekly digest.

Google Sheets, Knowledge Base, Outlook, Gmail
Why this matters Time management is most correlated with college success but least explicitly taught. Creates a commitment device — students follow plans they can see.
06

Communication Coach

Student says "Write an intro email to my professor" — agent drafts a professional email using appropriate tone, university context from KB, and presents for approval before sending.

Knowledge Base, Gmail, Outlook
Why this matters Professional email is hidden curriculum. Every drafted email is a model students learn from. Review-before-send keeps students in control.
07

Research Source Organizer

Student provides a topic and URLs — agent scrapes sources, creates an annotated bibliography in Docs, and builds a source tracking sheet with citation info and key quotes.

Firecrawl, Google Docs, Google Sheets
Why this matters Prevents the night-before-paper scramble. Bibliography-as-you-go supports outlining and makes research manageable.
08

Exam Prep Scheduler

Student says "CSE 310 midterm on March 15" — agent reads syllabus topics from KB, creates a study plan in Sheets with spaced repetition, schedules calendar blocks, and generates review doc templates.

Knowledge Base, Google Sheets, Outlook, Google Docs
Why this matters Implements evidence-based study strategies (spaced repetition) without requiring students to know the science. Converts "I should study more" into specific actions.
Tier 2: Near-Term

Minor Backend Additions

These need one or two targeted backend features but no architectural overhaul.

09

Assignment Tracker with Canvas Grades

Requires: Canvas API tool

Agent pulls real assignment data from Canvas, shows current standing, identifies at-risk courses, creates an action plan, and schedules extra study time for weak areas.

Why this matters Individual early warning system. "You need an 85 on the final to get a B" transforms passive grade-checking into active planning.
10

Personalized Learning Profile

Requires: Persistent student memory

Agent remembers courses, study preferences, and strengths/weaknesses across sessions. Each interaction builds on prior context rather than starting from zero.

Why this matters Context transforms the agent from a tool into a companion. The difference between a calculator and a tutor is memory.
11

Canvas Assignment Coach

Requires: Canvas API (rubrics)

Student says "Help me with Essay 2" — agent pulls the rubric, reads instructions from KB, asks clarifying questions, then creates an outline mapped to rubric criteria.

Why this matters Closes the gap between what students think they need to do and what the assignment actually requires. Maps intent to grading criteria explicitly.
12

Per-Student Tool Authentication

Requires: OAuth flow per student

Each student connects their own Google/Outlook account. Agents create files in the student's own Drive, events on their own calendar, send from their own email.

Why this matters The single most impactful backend investment. Makes all Tier 1 ideas production-ready and personal rather than demo-only.
Tier 3: Future Vision

Significant Investment

Longer-term possibilities that would differentiate the platform and represent the full potential of the system.

13

Multi-Course Conflict Resolver

Requires: Canvas API + persistent profile + advanced reasoning

Student says "I have 3 exams next week" — agent pulls all course data, identifies conflicts, proposes a prioritized study plan considering grade weights and current standing.

Why this matters Cross-course reasoning at scale. No human advisor has time to deliver this level of personalized triage to every student.
14

Career Prep Pipeline

Requires: Custom tools + persistent profile

Agent maintains a living resume, tracks skills per course, scrapes relevant internship postings, drafts tailored cover letters, and schedules career center appointments.

Why this matters Makes career development continuous rather than crisis-driven. Connects coursework to career outcomes in real time.
15

Wellbeing & Balance Monitor

Requires: Persistent profile + opt-in tracking

Agent notices patterns — overloaded weeks, missed sessions, declining engagement. Proactively suggests breaks, campus resources, and plan adjustments. Always opt-in, never diagnostic.

Why this matters Student mental health safety net without intrusion. "You've scheduled zero breaks this week" is supportive, not judgmental.
16

Peer Learning Network Builder

Requires: Cross-student matching + shared spaces

With consent, identifies peers in same courses, suggests study groups, creates shared collaboration spaces, and facilitates scheduling via calendar intersection.

Why this matters Belonging is the strongest predictor of persistence. Reduces social friction for commuter students, online learners, and introverts.
